Why GMAT prep gets interrupted (and why Parul kept restarting)

Parul came to the session as a working professional — the exact demographic most likely to abandon GMAT prep mid-cycle. Job demands, life events, motivation dips. She had attempted the GMAT multiple times, each restart carrying the emotional weight of 'starting over.' This is the story of how she broke that pattern.

How tracking metrics replaced motivation

Rather than rely on willpower to come back after a break, Parul shifted to tracking prep metrics: hours logged, mock scores, weak areas flagged. She stopped asking 'am I feeling motivated?' and started asking 'what do the metrics show I should do next?' This external anchor made restarts feel less like failures and more like data-driven course corrections.

The 760 came from structure, not inspiration

The final cycle wasn't longer or harder — it was more systematic. Daily prep blocks, weekly mock reviews, clear Q/V targets. No motivation required. Just discipline. When she hit Q50 V44, it wasn't a surprise — the metrics had been tracking toward it for weeks.
